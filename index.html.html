The Founder of the Maratha Empire: Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj


Chhatrapati Shivaji Shahaji Bhosale

[छत्रपती शिवाजी शहाजी भोसले]

(19 Feb 1630 - 3 Apr 1680)

Early life

Birth: Shivaji Maharaj was born on February 19, 1630, in the Shivneri Fort, Pune district.
Parents: Son of Shahaji Bhosale, a Maratha general, and Jijabai, who played a key role in shaping his vision of Swarajya (self-rule).
Inspiration: Inspired by his mother Jijabai and his surroundings to dream of an independent Maratha kingdom.

Establishment of Swarajya:

First Fort Capture: Shivaji captured the Torna Fort at the age of 16, marking the beginning of his kingdom.
Raigad as Capital: Made Raigad the capital of his empire after capturing several forts and expanding his territory.

Conflict with the Mughals:

Clash with Aurangzeb: Shivaji Maharaj resisted Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb's expansions in the Deccan region.
Escape from Agra: He famously escaped from Aurangzeb’s imprisonment in Agra in 1666, using clever disguise tactics.

Coronation as King :

Becoming Chhatrapati: In 1674, Shivaji was crowned as Chhatrapati (Emperor) at Raigad Fort, marking the official beginning of the Maratha Empire.


Shivrajyabhishek Sohola (Coronation Ceremony)

Religious Tolerance:

Respect for All Religions: Though a devout Hindu, Shivaji Maharaj was known for his respect towards other religions, particularly Islam, and ensured religious tolerance in his kingdom.

Legacy:

Death: Passed away on April 3, 1680, leaving behind a legacy of valor, leadership, and nation-building.
Inspirational Icon: Shivaji Maharaj's life continues to inspire millions in India, symbolizing the spirit of independence and resistance against foreign domination.
